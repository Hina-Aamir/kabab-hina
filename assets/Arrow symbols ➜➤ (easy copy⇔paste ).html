How to type simple arrow symbols on a keyboard (shortcuts)

Upwards Arrow ↑

  • ALT + 24 (hold the ALT button pressed and type the numbers 2 and 4)

Downwards Arrow ↓

  • ALT + 25 (hold the ALT button pressed and type the numbers 2 and 5)

Rightwards Arrow →

  • ALT + 26 (hold the ALT button pressed and type the numbers 2 and 6)

Leftwards Arrow ←

  • ALT + 27 (hold the ALT button pressed and type the numbers 2 and 7)

Left-Right Arrow ↔

  • ALT + 29 (hold the ALT button pressed and type the numbers 2 and 9)

Up-Down Arrow ↕

  • ALT + 18 (hold the ALT button pressed and type the numbers 1 and 8)

Meanings of arrow symbols

The arrow is one of the most known and universally used symbols since the 18th century. People use it to direct attention by pointing towards something or somewhere.

  • The upward arrow ↑ may indicate a forward direction. In scientific terms it may indicate an increase in something, while the downwards ↓ arrow may indicate a decrease.
  • The rightwards → arrow may indicate a direction to the right or that something leads to something else, for example, a chemical reaction.
  • The leftwards arrow ← may indicate a direction to the left or that something is derived from something else.
  • Other than the simple arrows may have a specific meaning in various sciences, where they're used to communicate the data and several diverse processes more effectively.
